Living In Ireland

I reached Ireland on the 5th of September 2016 and this is also my first time to step into this country. When I arrived

into the airport, a staff of ITB who picked me up from the airport. We have a nice conversation along the journey while

he was fetching me to the college's accommodation. When I reached to the accommodation, I settled down everything before going

out for exploring the city. My first accommodation in Ireland was in Sycamore BnB which is in Old Cabra Road. I met

my first friend in Ireland in Sycamore.That guy was staying with me in Sycamore. His name is Kunal from India and he

is also a student from ITB who studying Engineering. Kunal was accompanying me to explore the Dublin city during those few days

and showed me some basic introductions about using the infrastructure in the city. After a few days of staying in Old Cabra Road,

I finally moved into my exact accommodation in Blanchardstown which is where I am currently staying. I am staying with

an Irish family. My landlord is a very kind person, she treat us very well. She even cooked us some Irish traditional foods

for us occasionally. The college isnt too far from my accommodation therefore I used to walk everyday to the college.

There are few stuffs that I need time to get used to in Ireland which is the foods and weather. Despite I have been

used to the foods in Malaysia and it is all day long in the summer day in Malaysia. I feel freezing in the beginning

and not getting used to the Irish local food. In term of improving my Irish English, I tried to start up a conversation with

many Irish lads and it definately takes time for me to understand what are they trying to say in particularly they have their

Irish accent. In consequence, I prefer the life in Ireland and I hope I could get a job over here after I finished the study programme

in ITB.
